About L.I.W.A.N.A.G.

L.I.W.A.N.A.G. (Limb Inspection for Widespread Analysis of Neuropathy and Assessment for Guidance) is an innovative and automated robotic system designed for the early detection of diabetic foot ulcers (DFUs). This cost-effective device serves as an alternative to more expensive digital biothesiometers.

The system integrates several key functions to provide a comprehensive risk assessment and aims to address the critical gap in accessible early detection, particularly in underserved communities and low-resource healthcare settings.

Key Features

Vibration Perception Threshold (VPT)

Measures nerve sensitivity to detect neuropathy, a primary risk factor for DFUs.

Temperature Monitoring

Analyzes skin temperature differences to identify inflammation. A temperature difference of 2.2°C indicates inflammation.

Plantar Pressure Analysis

Monitors pressure distribution on the foot to pinpoint high-risk areas prone to developing ulcers.

Photoplethysmography (PPG)

Uses optical sensors to evaluate blood oxygen saturation (SpO2) and heart rate, providing insights into vascular health.

Our Mission

Our mission is to develop an affordable, efficient, and accessible diagnostic tool to combat the rising prevalence of diabetic foot complications. We aim to bridge the gap in preventative care, especially in low-income regions, by providing an integrated solution for the early detection of diabetic foot ulcers.

Through L.I.W.A.N.A.G., we strive to empower healthcare professionals, improve patient outcomes, and contribute to a more proactive and equitable approach to diabetes management.
